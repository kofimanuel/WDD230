Welcome to Tema, The "Harbour City"

Tema is a city located on the antlantic coast or southern part of Ghana. The city is 16 miles east of Ghana's capital, Accra in the greater Accra region. Tema is the capital of the Tema Metropolitan District. In 2013, the city became the eleventh most populous settlement in Ghana with a population of over 161, 000 people- a marked decrease from its 2005 figure of 209,000. The city is widely known for being a place where the Greenwich Meridian directly passes through. The city boasts of having one of the most busiest and largest seaports in the continent. For this reason the city is locally nicknamed the "Harbour City". There are 25 different communities in the city. The communities are numbered accordingly, communities One(1) through twenty five(5). Each community has basic access to social amenities. Located in the city are also other townships including Newtown, Sakumono, Ashaiman, Nungua, Teshie and more.

History

The city was initially built on the site of a small fishing village called "Torman", named for the local name of a calabash plant, "Tor", which was cultivated there. The village or town, "Man" was full of calabash plants, hence the name- Torman, town of calabash. The name change from Torman to Tema was due to the difficulties the British had pronouncing the name. When the city was constructed and its seaport commisioned, the Torman villagers migrated to a new fishing ground aroud 3 kilometres away. They later called this place Tema Newtown. The city was officially constructed and the harbour and port opened in 1962 by Ghana's first prime minister and president, Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah. Kwame Nkrumah turned the city city into an idustrial hub housing over 100 factories. The Tema Industrial Area serves as a ground for a number of key industries in the country and West Africa.
